### 1. You Don't Have to Be a Genius
* **The Myth of the Lone Genius:** Creativity is not the domain of isolated super-humans.
* **"Scenius":** Creativity happens in a "scene"â€”a collaborative ecology of talent. You don't need to be the smartest person in the room; you just need to contribute.
* **Embrace Amateurism:** Amateurs are willing to try anything and share the results. They are dangerous (in a good way) and authentic.

### 2. Think Process, Not Product
* **Behind-the-Scenes:** Audiences want to see *how* the sausage is made, not just the finished result.
* **Documentation:** Become a documentarian of your own life. Screen-record coding sessions, photograph sketches, and share drafts. Turn the invisible act of work into visible content.

### 3. Share Something Small Every Day
* **Stock vs. Flow:**
    * **Flow:** The daily feed (tweets, stories, quick posts) that keeps you relevant.
    * **Stock:** The durable content (books, apps, albums) that lasts.
* **The Daily Dispatch:** You don't need a manifesto daily. Share one small thingâ€”a quote, a sketch, or a snippet of code. Over a year, this builds a massive body of work.

### 4. Open Up Your Cabinet of Curiosities
* **Share Your Taste:** You are defined by what you love as much as by what you create.
* **Attribution:** "Credit is the currency of the internet." Always credit sources. Sharing your influences attracts your "tribe."
* **Don't Hoard:** Hoarding knowledge doesn't make you powerful; sharing it does.

### 5. Tell Good Stories
* **Context is King:** A project doesn't speak for itself. You must explain *why* you created it or the struggle involved.
* **Narrative Structure:** Use classic storytelling arcs. Where did you start? What dragon (obstacle) did you slay? What did you learn?

### 6. Teach What You Know
* **The Abundance Mindset:** Sharing "secrets" does not create competition; it creates *authority* and trust.
* **Educational Content:** Tutorials and "how-to" guides are the most shareable forms of content.

### 7. Don't Turn Into Human Spam
* **Listen to Be Heard:** To have fans, you must be a fan. Engage with others genuinely.
* **The Vampire Test:** If consuming someone's content drains you, they are a vampire. Don't be a vampire. Add energy to the conversation.

### 8. Learn to Take a Punch
* **Fear of Criticism:** The fear of judgment stops people from sharing.
* **Thick Skin:** The more you share, the more you get used to taking hits.
* **Trolls vs. Critics:** A critic cares about the work; a troll just wants to upset you. Ignore trolls; listen cautiously to critics.

### 9. Sell Out
* **Money Isn't Dirty:** It is okay to charge for your work.
* **Pass the Hat:** Once you build an audience by giving away value (flow), they will be happy to pay for finished products (stock).
* **Own Your List:** Social algorithms change. A subscriber list is the only asset you truly own. Prioritize it.

### 10. Stick Around
* **The Long Game:** Success is the result of showing up day after day for years.
* **Chain Smoking:** When you finish a project, use the momentum to light the next one immediately.
* **Sabbaticals:** Rest is fine, but the goal is always to come back.
